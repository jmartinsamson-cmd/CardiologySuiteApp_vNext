Effect of High-Intensity Statin Therapy on Cardiovascular Outcomes

January 15, 2024
John M. Smith, MD, PhD Sarah J. Johnson, MD

Study Summary

This multicenter randomized controlled trial evaluated the efficacy of high-intensity statin therapy in reducing major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) compared to moderate-intensity therapy in patients with established coronary artery disease.

Key Findings

  • 20% relative risk reduction in MACE with high-intensity therapy
  • LDL-C levels reduced by an additional 25 mg/dL on average
  • No significant increase in adverse events including myalgia

Clinical Implications

These findings support the use of high-intensity statin therapy as first-line treatment for secondary prevention in patients with established cardiovascular disease.

Study Details

The trial enrolled 5,234 patients across 127 centers in North America and Europe. Median follow-up was 3.2 years. Primary outcome was a composite of cardiovascular death, myocardial infarction, or stroke.
